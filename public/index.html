About Me

Gerard R. Bentley

Most Recently Research / Lab Manager at Pomona College working with FAIM Lab.

Claremont, CA, USA (Originally Warren, CT)

I'm a software developer interested in computer vision, web-based apps, ethical / explainable AI, and bringing coding to others.

I graduated from Pomona College in 2019 with a BA in Computer Science, then stuck around for the summer and fall as part of a post-bacc research year. Working with professor Joe Osborn we authored "The Videogame Affordances Corpus" at EXAG 2019, which was the first paper for our 'Formal Analysis of Interactive Media (FAIM) Lab'. My work over this year focused mainly on using Machine Learning techniques to analyze in-game screenshots from a collection of classic action and adventure games. I also spent my time developing tools for tagging data for the Computer Vision tasks, onboarding and managing tasks for a group of 6-10 undergraduate students in the lab, and building and setting up a 3-gpu linux workstation for training models.
